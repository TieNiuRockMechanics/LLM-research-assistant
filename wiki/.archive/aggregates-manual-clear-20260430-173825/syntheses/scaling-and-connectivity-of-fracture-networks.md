# Scaling and Connectivity of Fracture Networks

## Synthesis

Fracture networks exhibit scale-invariant (power-law) length distributions and fractal spatial clustering. Connectivity is controlled by percolation thresholds that depend on the interplay between length exponent and fractal dimension. The Universal Fracture Model unifies these observations by dividing networks into dilute and dense regimes. Permeability and transport behavior are strongly influenced by network topology, fracture intensity, and aperture-length correlations.
