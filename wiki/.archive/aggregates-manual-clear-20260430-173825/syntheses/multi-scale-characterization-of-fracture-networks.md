# Multi-Scale Characterization of Fracture Networks from Borehole and Outcrop Data

## Synthesis

Stereological relationships allow inference of 3D fracture geometry (diameter, density) from 1D scanlines or 2D traces. Borehole imaging tools have resolution limits (thickness <1mm, spacing <5mm) that underestimate fine fractures. Fractal and power-law models provide a framework for upscaling from core to reservoir scale. Machine learning methods (GANs, neural networks) now enable reconstruction and upscaling from single outcrop images.
