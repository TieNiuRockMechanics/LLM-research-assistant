# Multi-scale Fracture Network Characterization Using Fractal Geometry

## Synthesis

Fracture networks across scales (from microcracks to fault systems) exhibit fractal scaling in both spatial distribution and size distribution. The fractal dimension D and power-law exponent a are linked by relations such as a = D + 1 for self-similar networks, but deviations occur due to mechanical layering and tectonic history. These scaling laws enable upscaling from 1D borehole data to 2D/3D models, though stereological inversion remains challenging.
