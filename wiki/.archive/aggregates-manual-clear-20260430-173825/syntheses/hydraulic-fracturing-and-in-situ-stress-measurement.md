# Hydraulic Fracturing and In-Situ Stress Measurement

## Synthesis

Hydraulic fracturing theory couples poroelasticity, stress superposition, and fluid flow to predict breakdown and propagation behavior. It provides a method to infer in-situ stresses from pressure-time records. Field experiments (Rangely) demonstrate that fluid pressure control can induce or suppress seismicity, validating the effective stress principle for fault reactivation.
