# Flow Channeling and Non-Fickian Transport in Fracture Networks

## Synthesis

Flow in fracture networks is highly channelized; active flow paths occupy only a small fraction of the fracture surface. Dead-end fractures and aperture variability cause anomalous (non-Fickian) transport with power-law tailing. Network density, fracture length distribution, and aperture correlations control the degree of channeling. Upscaled models (CTRW) conditioned on velocity and tortuosity distributions can predict breakthrough curves.
