# Thermal Shock and Cooling-Induced Damage in Hot Dry Rock Geothermal Engineering

## Synthesis

Rapid cooling (e.g., by water or liquid nitrogen) of hot rock creates thermal gradients that induce microcracking, enhance permeability, and degrade mechanical strength. The effect depends on temperature, cooling rate, and rock type. Multiple cycles progressively worsen damage. These phenomena are exploited for reservoir stimulation but also pose risks to wellbore stability.
