# Discing Behavior and Mechanism of Cores Extracted from Songke-2 Well at Depths below 4,500 m.

## One-line Summary
This study uses 3D dynamic scanning to reconstruct fracture morphologies of disced cores from the Songke-2 well below 4,500 m, investigates fracture surface roughness and core intactness, and analyzes the mechanisms and stress paths of core discing. [Gao 2022, pp. 1-1]

## Research Question
What is the causal role of fracture surface topography in disced fracture, and what are the mechanisms and characteristics of core discing along stress paths in a deep borehole (>4,500 m)? [Gao 2022, pp. 1-1, 3-3]

## Study Area / Data
- **Site:** Songke-2 well (Songliao Basin, NE China), part of the International Continental Scientific Drilling Program (ICDP) Cretaceous Continental Scientific Drilling Project. Total depth: 7018 m; coring began at 4000 m. [Gao 2022, pp. 3-4]
- **Target depths and lithologies for disced cores:**
  - **Group A (17 discs):** 5724.63–5744.61 m, sedimentary rock (Huoshiling Formation).
  - **Group B (8 discs):** 5724.63–5744.61 m, sedimentary rock (Huoshiling Formation).
  - **Group C (7 discs):** 6430.80–6441.65 m, igneous rock (Basement). [Gao 2022, pp. 4-5]
- The strata where disced cores occur are mainly the Shahezi Formation and the Huoshiling Formation. [Gao 2022, pp. 3-4]

## Methods
- **3D fracture morphology reconstruction:** Three-dimensional dynamic scanning technology was used to reconstruct fracture surfaces of disced cores. [Gao 2022, pp. 1-1]
- **Roughness quantification:** Fracture surface roughness was calculated using an improved cubic covering method to estimate the fractal dimension (D) of spatial geometric morphology. Multiple 1 mm × 1 mm squares were analyzed on different regions of the fracture surfaces using a CATIA reconstruction platform. [Gao 2022, pp. 6-7]
- **Core intactness metric:** A parametric integrity coefficient of disced rock sample (I_DRS) was defined as:
  I_DRS = (true volume of disced region) / (volume of cylinder with the longest generatrix) × 100%. [Gao 2022, pp. 9-10]
- **Mechanism analysis:** The mechanisms and characteristics of fractures along stress paths were investigated, linking energy dissipation principles to fracture type (tension vs. shear) and propagation speed. [Gao 2022, pp. 8-9]

## Key Findings
- **Saddle-shaped fractures in shallow cores:** Shallow disced cores exhibit notable saddle-shaped coarse fractures, which may relate to rock properties. [Gao 2022, pp. 1-1]
- **Smoother fractures in deep igneous rock:** Fracture surfaces of deep igneous rock samples (>6000 m) are smoother than those from relatively shallow strata. This is attributed to higher residual elastic energy in deep, high in-situ stress environments, causing faster and more direct fracture propagation. [Gao 2022, pp. 8-8]
- **Roughness gradient on sedimentary cores:** For sedimentary cores (Huoshiling Fm., 5725–5735 m), fractal dimension D and roughness of both the upside and downside fracture surfaces gradually increase from the inside to the outside. The relatively high roughness on the outside may relate to sedimentary bedding and heterogeneity. [Gao 2022, pp. 8-9]
- **Fracture initiation and propagation:** Smooth edges on deep cores (>6000 m) suggest that core discing is mainly controlled by shear forces with significant energy dissipation and relatively low fracture speeds. An increase in roughness in the central region indicates a transformation from mode II to mode I cracks (dominated by tension). However, the centermost roughness remains relatively low, suggesting that core fracture may be affected by external factors such as drilling disturbance. Fracture propagation likely initiates at the edge. [Gao 2022, pp. 9-10]
- **Thin disc sequences:** The presence of thin, continuous disc sequences within a small area is unrelated to depth and lithology. Relatively complete core sections above and below these thin discs suggest a direct relation to the drilling and coring processes. [Gao 2022, pp. 4-6]
- **Continuous vs. inhomogeneous discing:** Core samples from the Huoshiling Formation exhibit typical continuous series of thin rock discs (Group A), while other groups show inwardly concave fracture surfaces and inhomogeneous thicknesses (Group B) or horizontal fracture surfaces with inhomogeneous thicknesses (Group C). [Gao 2022, pp. 4-5]

## Limitations
- The relationship between fracture surface topography and causal mechanisms of discing is described as “not always” clear, and this study represents a step toward addressing that. [Gao 2022, pp. 3-3]
- The calculation of the integrity coefficient I_DRS was defined, but its validation or applicability beyond this well is not detailed in the provided fragments.
- The analysis is based on a single well; the generalizability of the specific morphological patterns to other tectonic settings may be limited.

## Assumptions / Conditions
- **In-situ stress regime:** The study analyzes discing under varying initial in-situ stress field factor ratios (σ_V : σ_H : σ_h), tested between 1:0.50:0.50 and 1:2.00:2.00. [Gao 2022, pp. 7-8]
- **Stress path during failure:** Regardless of the initial stress state, during drilling, radial stress is released first, followed by axial stress; the final failure stress state is always uniaxial. [Gao 2022, pp. 8-8]
- **Energy-fracture relationship:** The minimum energy density for discing-induced fracture is defined as E_fmin = σ² / 2E for tensile failure and E_fmin = σ_τ² / 2G for shear failure. [Gao 2022, pp. 8-8]
- **Fracture surface and speed:** Higher fracture speeds are assumed to produce rougher surfaces, based on Boudet et al. [Gao 2022, pp. 9-10]

## Key Variables / Parameters
- **Fractal Dimension (D):** Quantifies fracture spatial geometric morphology and surface roughness. Calculated using an improved cubic covering method. [Gao 2022, pp. 6-7]
- **Integrity Coefficient of Disced Rock Sample (I_DRS):** Ratio (%) of the true volume of the disced region to the volume of the cylinder with the longest generatrix. [Gao 2022, pp. 9-10]
- **Minimum Energy Density (E_fmin):** Defined by formulas (1), (2), and (3) for residual in-situ stress (σ_i), tensile failure stress (σ_t), and shear failure stress (σ_τ). [Gao 2022, pp. 8-8]
- **In-situ stress factor ratios:** Cases 1–28 define variations among vertical (σ_V), maximum horizontal (σ_H), and minimum horizontal (σ_h) stresses. [Gao 2022, pp. 7-8]

## Reusable Claims
1.  **Claim:** In deep, high in-situ stress environments, fracture surfaces of igneous rocks are smoother than those of rocks from shallower depths. **Condition:** For Songke-2 well cores from depths >6000 m vs. relatively shallow sedimentary formations. **Evidence:** 3D dynamic scanning and fractal dimension analysis show smoother surfaces on deep igneous cores. [Gao 2022, pp. 8-8] **Limitation:** Claim is specific to the contrast observed between the deep igneous basement and the shallower Huoshiling Formation at this single well.
2.  **Claim:** The final stress state at failure for a core during drilling is always uniaxial, regardless of the initial in-situ stress state. **Condition:** During the drilling and coring process, radial stress is released before axial stress. **Evidence:** Theoretical analysis of stress paths. [Gao 2022, pp. 8-8] **Limitation:** A simplifying assumption for developing the energy-based model.
3.  **Claim:** On disced sedimentary rock cores, surface roughness (measured by fractal dimension D) increases from the center to the edge. **Condition:** Sedimentary rocks (e.g., from Huoshiling Formation at 5725–5735 m in Songke-2 well), assessed using the same-scale nonconcentric method. **Evidence:** Fractal dimension D values increase from inner to outer regions on both upside and downside surfaces. [Gao 2022, pp. 8-9] **Limitation:** May be influenced by sedimentary bedding and heterogeneity; not tested on igneous samples.
4.  **Claim:** The presence of a sequence of thin, continuous core discs is directly related to the drilling and coring process rather than depth or lithology. **Condition:** Observed in Songke-2 well and China Jinping Underground Laboratory, where relatively complete core sections exist directly above and below the thin discs. **Evidence:** Comparative morphology of recovered cores. [Gao 2022, pp. 4-6] **Limitation:** The exact process-related mechanism is stated as requiring further investigation.
5.  **Claim:** A method for quantifying the intactness of saddle-shaped disced cores is defined by the parameter I_DRS. **Condition:** Applicable to cores with a cylindrical form, where I_DRS = (true volume of disced region) / (volume of cylinder with longest generatrix) × 100%. **Evidence:** Definition and schematic provided in the paper. [Gao 2022, pp. 9-10] **Limitation:** Performance and sensitivity of this parameter across different rock types or discing morphologies were not reported in the fragments.

- **Core discing as a stress indicator:** The study notes that disced cores have historically been used as indicators of high in-situ stress, but that discing can also occur in low-stress environments. [Gao 2022, pp. 1-2]
