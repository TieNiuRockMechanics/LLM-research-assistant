# Technical Note: Development Program of Hot Dry Rock Geothermal Resource in the Yangbajing Basin of China.

## One-line Summary
This paper proposes a development program for the hot dry rock (HDR) geothermal resource in the Yangbajing Basin, China, estimating a massive resource potential and designing an extraction system using natural tectonic faults as an artificial reservoir.

## Research Question
How can the deep-seated hot dry rock geothermal resource in the Yangbajing Basin be effectively and economically extracted, considering the region's geological structure, in situ stress field, and temperature distribution?

## Study Area / Data
The study focuses on the Yangbajing Basin in Tibet, China, specifically the Dangxiong–Yangbajing Basin located southeast of the Nyainqêntanglha Range. Data sources include:
- Geophysical exploration data indicating a liquating magma chamber at 5–15 km depth with an outer temperature of ~500°C [Feng 2012, pp. 2-2].
- Crustal stress measurements using the piezo-magnetic stress-relief method at four test points (Ybj1-Ybj4) [Feng 2012, pp. 2-3].
- Focal mechanism solution analyses of earthquakes in the region [Feng 2012, pp. 2-3].
- Temperature distribution analysis from finite element modeling [Feng 2012, pp. 2-3].
- Isotope analysis of thermal groundwater [Feng 2012, pp. 2-2].

## Methods
- **Temperature Field Analysis:** The finite element method was used to analyze the zonal temperature distribution of the geothermal field, based on the temperature of the deep-seated liquating rock mass (>500°C) [Feng 2012, pp. 2-3].
- **Stress Field Characterization:** Crustal stress measurements and focal mechanism solution analyses were conducted to determine the magnitude and orientation of the in situ stress field [Feng 2012, pp. 2-3].
- **Resource Estimation:** The total geothermal reserve was calculated using the volume of the liquating mass, its average temperature, and a lowest extractable temperature of 150°C [Feng 2012, pp. 2-3].
- **Extraction System Design:** A program was designed using a vertical injection well and two inclined production wells intersecting natural fault zones to create an artificial reservoir [Feng 2012, pp. 3-5].
- **Seepage Analysis:** The coefficient of seepage in fissures was calculated using a formula relating it to stress and water pressure [Feng 2012, pp. 3-5].
- **Investment Analysis:** Costs for drilling, power station construction, and other expenses were estimated [Feng 2012, pp. 5-6].

## Key Findings
1.  The HDR geothermal resource in the Yangbajing region is estimated at 5.4 × 10^9 MWa, representing a huge potential energy source for China [Feng 2012, pp. 2-3].
2.  The minimum horizontal principal stress is oriented normal to the strike of the basin and the fault planes, while the maximum horizontal principal stress is parallel to the strike [Feng 2012, pp. 2-3].
3.  A development program is proposed: a vertical injection well drilled into fault F1 and two inclined production wells intersecting faults F1–F5, creating an artificial reservoir of 3 × 10^11 m³ [Feng 2012, pp. 3-5].
4.  This proposed reservoir volume is 360 times larger than the hydrofractured reservoir in Cornwall, UK [Feng 2012, pp. 5-6].
5.  An initial 1000 MW capacity power station could be constructed for approximately 1 billion yuan, producing 8.64 × 10^9 kWh per year [Feng 2012, pp. 5-6].
6.  The system could support a 10,000 MW power station with an operating life of at least 100 years [Feng 2012, pp. 5-6].

## Limitations
- The extraction program relies on the existence and connectivity of natural fault zones (F1–F5) as the primary reservoir and flow paths.
- The economic analysis is preliminary and based on estimated costs for drilling and construction.
- The long-term performance and sustainability of the artificial reservoir under continuous extraction are not confirmed.
- The thermal use ratio of 17% is an assumption for electricity generation efficiency [Feng 2012, pp. 5-6].

## Assumptions / Conditions
- The deep-seated liquating magma chamber is the primary heat source for the HDR resource.
- The natural fault zones (F1–F5) are sufficiently permeable and interconnected to allow fluid circulation between the injection and production wells.
- The minimum horizontal principal stress being normal to the fault planes will keep existing and newly formed fissures open and intersecting the production wells.
- The geothermal gradient above the magma chamber is approximately 45°C/km [Feng 2012, pp. 2-3].
- The density of granite is 2700 kg/m³ and its specific heat capacity is 1000 J/kg/°C for resource calculations [Feng 2012, pp. 5-6].

## Key Variables / Parameters
- **Geothermal Gradient:** ~45°C/km [Feng 2012, pp. 2-3].
- **Temperature of Liquating Mass:** >500°C [Feng 2012, pp. 2-3].
- **Maximum Horizontal Principal Stress:** 3.3–10.4 MPa, direction NE–NEE [Feng 2012, pp. 2-3].
- **Minimum Horizontal Principal Stress:** 2.5–8.4 MPa [Feng 2012, pp. 2-3].
- **Artificial Reservoir Volume:** 3 × 10^11 m³ [Feng 2012, pp. 5-6].
- **Thermal Use Ratio (Efficiency):** 0.17 (17%) [Feng 2012, pp. 5-6].
- **Drilling Price (P):** 8000 yuan/m [Feng 2012, pp. 5-6].

## Reusable Claims
- **Claim:** Utilizing pre-existing tectonic fault zones as artificial geothermal reservoirs can significantly reduce the cost and technical difficulty associated with deep horizontal drilling for HDR extraction.
  - **Condition:** This approach is applicable in regions with well-characterized, deep, permeable fault systems oriented favorably relative to the in situ stress field.
  - **Limitation:** The method depends on the natural connectivity and stress-dependent permeability of the fault zones.
- **Claim:** The Yangbajing Basin possesses a HDR geothermal resource estimated at 5.4 × 10^9 MWa, with the potential to support a power station of 10,000 MW capacity for over 100 years.
  - **Condition:** This estimate is based on a specific geological model of a liquating magma chamber and assumes a lowest extractable temperature of 150°C.
  - **Limitation:** The actual extractable resource may be influenced by heat recharge from the magma chamber and the efficiency of the heat exchange system.
