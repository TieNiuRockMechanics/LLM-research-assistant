# Experimental Study on the Acoustic Emission Characteristics of Non-Uniform Deformation Evolution of Granite under Cyclic Loading and Unloading Test.

## One-line Summary
This study investigates the relationship between acoustic emission (AE) signals and the evolution of non-uniform deformation in granite specimens during uniaxial cyclic loading and unloading tests.

## Research Question
How do the acoustic emission characteristics correspond to the evolution of non-uniform deformation in rock materials during a cyclic loading process?

## Study Area / Data
The study used granite specimens with dimensions of 50 mm × 50 mm × 100 mm [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4]. The uniaxial cyclic loading tests were conducted with loading stress levels of 15, 30, 45, 60, 75, and 90 MPa, with a loading rate of 0.1 kN/s [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4].

## Methods
The experimental system combined an acoustic emission (AE) system and a CCD camera to collect AE signals and surface deformation images during the deformation and failure process [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4]. The digital speckle correlation method was used to analyze the deformation field evolution [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4]. The AE system used three acquisition probes with a threshold of 40 dB and a gain of 50 dB, and a sampling rate of 2 MHz [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4]. The CCD camera captured images at a rate of 2 frames per second with a resolution of 1600 × 1200 pixels [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4].

## Key Findings
1.  The minimum point of non-uniform deformation (Sw) corresponded to the lowest point of unloading stress during each cyclic loading process [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4].
2.  After the startup of the localisation band, the maximum point of non-uniform deformation lagged behind the vertex of loading stress in each cyclic loading process [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4].
3.  The first turning and increasing point of non-uniform deformation (Sw) is the starting point of the increase of the AE signal, whereas the maximum point of non-uniform deformation is the starting point of the quiet period of the AE signal [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4].
4.  With the increase of cyclic number, the degree of non-uniform deformation (Sw) is negatively correlated with the Felicity ratio; greater non-uniform deformation corresponds to greater damage and a smaller Felicity ratio [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4].

## Limitations
The study was conducted under uniaxial compression conditions only. The research focused on a single rock type (granite). The analysis of non-uniform deformation was based on surface measurements via digital image correlation.

## Assumptions / Conditions
The tests assumed uniaxial stress conditions. The loading protocol followed a specific cyclic pattern with fixed stress levels (15, 30, 45, 60, 75, 90 MPa) and a constant loading rate of 0.1 kN/s [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4]. The AE system parameters (threshold, gain, sampling rate) were fixed for all tests [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4].

## Key Variables / Parameters
*   **Non-uniform deformation (Sw):** A parameter quantifying the degree of heterogeneity in the deformation field, calculated from the standard deviation of displacement fields [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4].
*   **Acoustic Emission (AE) Signal:** Characterized by ringing counts and energy [Yang 2018, pp. 4-7].
*   **Felicity Ratio (F):** Defined as the ratio of the stress at which AE resumes in a subsequent cycle to the maximum stress of the previous cycle (F = σ_i / σ_{i-1}) [Yang 2018, pp. 4-7].
*   **Cyclic Loading Stress Level:** The maximum stress applied in each loading cycle (15, 30, 45, 60, 75, 90 MPa) [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4].

## Reusable Claims
1.  In granite under cyclic uniaxial loading, the onset of increasing acoustic emission activity coincides with the first turning point in the non-uniform deformation parameter (Sw) [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4].
2.  A quiet period in acoustic emission signals begins at the point of maximum non-uniform deformation (Sw) during a loading cycle [Yang 2018, pp. 1-4].
3.  The Felicity ratio exhibits a negative correlation with the magnitude of non-uniform deformation (Sw) measured at the unloading stress minimum, indicating its potential as a damage index [Yang 2018, pp. 4-7].
