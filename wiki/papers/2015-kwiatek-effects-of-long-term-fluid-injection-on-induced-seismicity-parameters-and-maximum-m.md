# Effects of Long-Term Fluid Injection on Induced Seismicity Parameters and Maximum Magnitude in Northwestern Part of The Geysers Geothermal Field.

## One-line Summary
This study analyzes seven years of induced seismicity near two injection wells at The Geysers geothermal field, finding that long-term fluid injection causes seismicity to migrate deeper, correlates with injection rates, and suggests maximum magnitude is constrained by the seismic cloud's geometry and pore pressure changes rather than total injected volume.

## Research Question
How do long-term temporal and spatial changes in the statistical, source, and stress characteristics of induced seismicity relate to field operations, fluid migration, and constraints on the maximum likely magnitude in the northwestern part of The Geysers geothermal field? [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 1-1]

## Study Area / Data
The study focuses on a distinct cluster of induced seismicity in the northwestern part of The Geysers geothermal field, California, associated with injection wells Prati-9 and Prati-29. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 2-4]
The seismicity catalog analyzed consists of 1776 events recorded over nearly 7 years (November 2007 to August 2014) as reported by the Northern California Earthquake Data Center (NCEDC). [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 4-5]
Between November 2007 and August 2014, about 10.5 Mm³ of treated wastewater was injected into wells Prati-9 (7.21 Mm³) and Prati-29 (3.29 Mm³). [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 2-4]
The microseismicity was recorded by a local seismic network of 31 three-component short-period geophones operated by Lawrence-Berkeley National Laboratory. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 2-4]

## Methods
The initial hypocenter catalog was refined using the double-difference relocation technique to improve internal precision down to approximately 50 m. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 4-5]
Focal mechanisms were recalculated with the HASH software using first-motion polarities. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 4-5]
Source parameters were initially estimated using the spectral fitting method and then refined using the mesh spectral ratio technique to reduce attenuation effects. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 5-6]
Long-term temporal variations were investigated by splitting the catalog into overlapping time windows to calculate cluster characteristics, b-values, spatial ellipsoid volumes, faulting type proportions, and stress tensor orientations. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 5-6]
The stress tensor and shape ratio R were inverted using the MSATSI software in moving time windows of 55 events. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 5-6]

## Key Findings
Seismicity hypocenters migrated toward greater depths over time, a process most intense during combined injection into both wells. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 7-8]
The volume of the seismic cloud (subcluster A) correlated with injection rates, expanding during high-rate winter injection and contracting after the shut-in of Prati-29. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 7-8]
The average Gutenberg-Richter b-value was 1.22 ± 0.08, with no observable long-term trend. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 8-10]
The maximum observed moment magnitude was M_W 3.2 (or M_W 3.4 after spectral ratio refinement), significantly lower than the M_W 5.5 predicted by the McGarr (2014) relation for the total injected volume. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 8-10]
Larger events (M_W > 2.5) and their fault planes were contained within the seismic cloud boundaries, unlike some other studies where large events occur at the cloud margins. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 10-11]
The observed maximum magnitude was consistent with models relating it to the dimensions of the seismic cloud (e.g., the shortest ellipsoid axis) or to the product of the weakened formation volume and average pore pressure change. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 13-14]

## Limitations
The study acknowledges that the physics governing induced seismicity are still poorly understood. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 1-2]
The seismic efficiency in hydrothermal systems like The Geysers is low, making direct application of relations like McGarr (2014) problematic due to fluid vaporization and high mobility. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 14-15]
The analysis assumes a 1-D velocity model for focal mechanism calculations. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 4-5]
The influence of production from nearby wells (Prati-4 and Prati-5) on the analyzed seismicity characteristics was considered negligible but not definitively ruled out. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 2-4]

## Assumptions / Conditions
The magnitude of completeness (Mc) for the catalog is about M_W 1.4 (M_D 1.0). [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 4-5]
For source parameter calculations, assumed P-wave velocity V_P = 4785 m/s, S-wave velocity V_S = 2848 m/s (V_P/V_S = 1.68), and density ρ = 2700 kg/m³. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 5-6]
Radiation pattern corrections R_P = 0.52 and R_S = 0.63 were applied for P and S waves. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 5-6]
The Madariaga (1976) circular source model with scaling constant k = 2.01 was used to calculate source radius. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 5-6]
The stress inversion assumed a constant vertical stress (S_V = S_1) with pore pressure variations. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 11-11]

## Reusable Claims
- In a vapor-dominated geothermal reservoir, long-term fluid injection causes induced seismicity to migrate to greater depths over time, with the migration rate increasing during periods of higher combined injection rates. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 7-8]
- The spatial volume of an induced seismicity cluster can be positively correlated with the average fluid injection rate, expanding during high-rate injection and contracting during shut-in periods. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 13-14]
- The maximum magnitude of induced seismicity in a confined geothermal reservoir may be better constrained by the geometric dimensions of the seismic cloud (e.g., its shortest axis) or by the product of the weakened formation volume and average pore pressure increase, rather than by the total volume of injected fluid. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 13-15]
- In hydrothermal systems, the seismic efficiency is low, and the maximum observed magnitude can be orders of magnitude lower than predictions based on total injected volume and shear modulus, due to factors like fluid vaporization and high fluid mobility. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 14-15]
- Large induced events (M_W > 2.5) can be contained within the boundaries of the seismic cloud, contrasting with observations from some other sites where they occur at the cloud margins. [Kwiatek 2015, pp. 10-11]
