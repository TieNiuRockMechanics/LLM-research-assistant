# Gao wen jun yun ya li hua gang yan re po lie sheng fa she te xing shi yan yan jiu

## One-line Summary
This study experimentally investigates the acoustic emission (AE) characteristics and thermal cracking behavior of large granite samples under high temperature (up to 500°C) and isostatic stress (25 MPa).

## Research Question
The research aims to obtain the rules of thermal cracking on granite and analyze the evolution of acoustic emission characteristics within the temperature range from room temperature to 500°C under isostatic stress, discussing the rock failure characteristics reflected by AE signals at each stage [Wu 2012, pp. 1-2].

## Study Area / Data
The study uses two large cylindrical samples (φ200 mm × 400 mm) of "Luhui granite" collected from Pingyi, Shandong Province, China. The granite is gray-white and dense, with mineral composition determined by XRD: illite (25%), quartz (28%), feldspar (43%), calcite (1%), siderite (1%), and others (remainder) [Wu 2012, pp. 1-2].

## Methods
The experiments were conducted using a "600°C, 20 MN servo-controlled high-temperature high-pressure rock triaxial testing machine" developed by China University of Mining and Technology. The system applies isostatic stress via solid pressure transmission, with independent axial and lateral loading. Temperature was measured with thermocouples. Acoustic emission was monitored using a PAC DiSP 24-channel AE system with 4 channels, sampling at 100 Msps and a preamplifier gain of 60 dB. The experimental procedure involved: 1) installing the sample in the triaxial cell, 2) debugging systems, 3) applying the preset pressure (axial and confining pressure both 25 MPa) and collecting noise AE, and 4) heating at a rate of about 5°C/h while collecting AE data [Wu 2012, pp. 2-5].

## Key Findings
1.  **330°C is a critical demarcation point** for AE and thermal cracking behavior. Below 330°C, thermal cracking is elastic rupture. Above 330°C, local plastic deformation and failure occur due to the metamorphism or melting of illite (25% content) and differences in thermal expansion coefficients of minerals [Wu 2012, pp. 1-2, 5-7].
2.  **AE characteristics change above 330°C**. Local plastic failure generates a large number of low-energy-release AE events. The AE concentrated zones consist of a small number of high-energy-rate events and a large number of low-energy-rate events, resulting in lower overall cumulative energy release compared to temperatures below 330°C [Wu 2012, pp. 1-2, 5-7].
3.  **Abrupt changes in AE counts rate** indicate the formation of micro-fracture zones. The granite experienced two significant improvements in its thermal crack network starting from temperatures of 110°C and 420°C, respectively [Wu 2012, pp. 1-2, 5-7].
4.  **AE phenomena are periodic**, reflecting a cyclic process of energy accumulation and release in the thermal cracking of rock [Wu 2012, pp. 5-7].

## Limitations
The study notes that differences in AE signals between the two samples exist due to factors like different AE threshold settings (60 dB vs. 40 dB), the large size and heterogeneity of the samples, and non-uniform distribution of original micro-cracks and thermal stress [Wu 2012, pp. 5-7]. The AE system did not record waveforms, preventing post-acquisition noise filtering [Wu 2012, pp. 2-5].

## Assumptions / Conditions
The experiments were conducted under a constant isostatic stress of 25 MPa. The heating rate was approximately 5°C/h. The granite samples were assumed to be representative of the "Luhui granite" from Pingyi, Shandong [Wu 2012, pp. 1-2, 2-5].

## Key Variables / Parameters
- **Temperature**: Range from room temperature to 500°C.
- **Isostatic Stress**: 25 MPa (both axial and confining pressure).
- **Sample Size**: φ200 mm × 400 mm.
- **AE Parameters**: Counts rate (振铃率), energy rate (能量率), duration (持续时间), amplitude (振幅).
- **Critical Temperature**: 330°C (demarcation point).

## Reusable Claims
1.  **Condition**: For Luhui granite under 25 MPa isostatic stress. **Claim**: The temperature of 330°C marks a transition in thermal cracking mechanism from elastic rupture to a regime involving local plastic deformation and failure, which is associated with the thermal behavior of illite and differential mineral expansion [Wu 2012, pp. 1-2, 5-7].
2.  **Condition**: During thermal cracking of granite above 330°C. **Claim**: Acoustic emission activity is characterized by concentrated zones comprising a mix of high-energy and numerous low-energy events, leading to a lower net cumulative energy release compared to lower temperature ranges [Wu 2012, pp. 1-2, 5-7].
3.  **Condition**: In granite under coupled thermal and stress loading. **Claim**: An abrupt increase in the acoustic emission counts rate can serve as an indicator for the initiation of micro-fracture zone formation and the onset of significant improvements in the rock's crack network [Wu 2012, pp. 1-2, 5-7].
