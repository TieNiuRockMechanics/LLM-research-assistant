# Characteristics of Granite Failure by Injecting High-Temperature-Vapour under Uniaxial Stress.

## One-line Summary
High-temperature vapor injection under uniaxial stress significantly weakens granite strength and reduces breakdown pressure compared to room-temperature hydraulic fracturing, with failure proceeding through thermal fracture damage and macrocrack propagation stages.

## Research Question
The study aims to reveal the hydraulic fracture mechanism of rock under high temperature and pressure, specifically investigating the failure characteristics of granite when injected with high-temperature vapor under uniaxial stress [Wu 2019, pp. 1-3].

## Study Area / Data
The experiments used granite specimens with dimensions of 120 mm × 120 mm × 300 mm, containing a central borehole of φ20 mm × 180 mm. The uniaxial compressive strength of the granite was 86 MPa, and the tensile strength was 6 MPa [Wu 2019, pp. 1-3].

## Methods
High-temperature-vapour-driven failure experiments were conducted on granite under uniaxial stress. Vapor was injected at temperatures of 350°C, 400°C, 430°C, and 450°C at different rates. The injection pressure, lateral deformation (in x, y, z directions), and acoustic emission (AE) characteristics were monitored. The experimental system included a true triaxial heat-injection apparatus and a PAC PCI-2 acoustic emission system [Wu 2019, pp. 1-3].

## Key Findings
1.  High temperature greatly weakens granite strength and reduces breakdown pressure. Compared to hydraulic fracturing at room temperature, breakdown pressure decreased by at least 58% at high injection rates (430°C and 350°C vapor) and by 75% at low injection rates (400°C and 450°C vapor) [Wu 2019, pp. 1-3].
2.  The failure process can be divided into two stages: thermal fracture damage and macrocrack propagation. In the first stage, thermal stress causes random thermal cracks around the borehole. In the second stage, fractures primarily generate on both sides of the borehole and propagate along the final macrocrack trace until specimen failure [Wu 2019, pp. 1-3].
3.  Failure induced by vapor at a low injection rate is a slow ductile tensile failure. The fracture extends asymmetrically along the borehole, and its width is smaller than that from hydraulic fracturing at room temperature [Wu 2019, pp. 1-3].
4.  Acoustic emission (AE) activity concentrated in the final stage of failure, with over 90% of AE events occurring in the last 150-200 seconds before specimen failure [Wu 2019, pp. 3-7].

## Limitations
The study was conducted under uniaxial stress conditions, which may not fully represent the true triaxial stress state in deep geothermal reservoirs. The experiments were preliminary, and the failure mechanism under thermo-mechanical coupling requires further investigation [Wu 2019, pp. 1-3].

## Assumptions / Conditions
The experiments assumed that the failure mechanism under high-temperature vapor injection could be studied under simplified uniaxial stress conditions. The granite specimens were assumed to be representative of hot dry rock reservoirs [Wu 2019, pp. 1-3].

## Reusable Claims
-   Under uniaxial stress, injecting high-temperature vapor (350-450°C) into granite reduces its breakdown pressure by 58-75% compared to room-temperature hydraulic fracturing [Wu 2019, pp. 1-3].
-   The failure process of granite under high-temperature vapor injection consists of two distinct stages: initial thermal fracture damage around the borehole followed by macrocrack propagation [Wu 2019, pp. 1-3].
-   Low-rate high-temperature vapor injection induces a slow, ductile tensile failure in granite, characterized by asymmetric fracture propagation and a narrower fracture width than conventional hydraulic fracturing [Wu 2019, pp. 1-3].
-   Acoustic emission monitoring indicates that the majority of damage events occur rapidly in the final stage of specimen failure under high-temperature vapor injection [Wu 2019, pp. 3-7].
