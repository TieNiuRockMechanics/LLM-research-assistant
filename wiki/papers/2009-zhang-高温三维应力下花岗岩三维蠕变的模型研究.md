# 高温三维应力下花岗岩三维蠕变的模型研究.

## One-line Summary
This study experimentally investigates the three-dimensional creep behavior of Lu Gray granite under high temperature (up to 600°C) and triaxial stress, proposing a Burgers viscoelastic model to describe both axial and volumetric creep.

## Research Question
The research aims to verify whether hydrostatic stress causes volumetric creep (contrary to some classical theories), characterize the creep curve features of granite under high-temperature 3D stress, and determine a suitable viscoelastic model for its volumetric creep deformation [Zhang 2009, pp. 1-3].

## Study Area / Data
The study uses large cylindrical specimens (φ200 mm × 400 mm) of fresh Lu Gray granite from a quarry in Pingyi, Shandong, China [Zhang 2009, pp. 1-3]. Experiments were conducted using the "20 MN servo-controlled triaxial rock testing machine with high temperature and high pressure" at China University of Mining and Technology [Zhang 2009, pp. 1-3].

## Methods
- **Experimental Setup:** A 20 MN servo-controlled triaxial testing machine was used to apply high temperature (up to 600°C) and high axial stress (up to 175 MPa) to large granite specimens [Zhang 2009, pp. 1-3].
- **Loading Conditions:** Tests included axial creep under differential stress (e.g., 300°C, σ1=94 MPa, σ2=σ3=75 MPa; 500°C, σ1=175 MPa, σ2=σ3=125 MPa) and volumetric creep under hydrostatic stress (e.g., 300°C, 500°C, 600°C at σ1=σ2=σ3=125 MPa) [Zhang 2009, pp. 3-5].
- **Measurement:** Axial and lateral displacements were measured using a grating ruler and dial gauges with a precision of 0.005 mm [Zhang 2009, pp. 1-3].
- **Modeling Approach:** The Burgers model (a Kelvin body in series with a Maxwell body) was selected to simulate the creep curves. Model parameters were determined by analyzing the instantaneous elastic strain, steady-state creep rate, and the transient creep stage [Zhang 2009, pp. 5-7].

## Key Findings
1.  Under 3D stress, the axial creep at 300°C and 500°C, and the volumetric creep at 300°C, 500°C, and 600°C all exhibit three distinct stages: primary (transient) creep, steady-state creep, and accelerating creep [Zhang 2009, pp. 1-3].
2.  The volume, length, and radius of the granite specimens increased with creep time due to thermal cracking generating numerous internal microcracks. Lateral deformation grew faster than axial deformation [Zhang 2009, pp. 1-3, pp. 5-7].
3.  The basic hypothesis that hydrostatic stress causes volumetric creep and differential stress causes axial creep was used to derive the volumetric creep constitutive equation for a Burgers model under 3D stress [Zhang 2009, pp. 1-3].
4.  The Burgers model adequately simulates the axial creep at 300°C and 500°C, and the volumetric creep at 300°C, 500°C, and 600°C, with model parameters successfully calculated [Zhang 2009, pp. 1-3].

## Limitations
- The study acknowledges that classical rock creep theories generally assume volumetric deformation is elastic and does not exhibit creep, which this work challenges [Zhang 2009, pp. 3-5].
- The test duration for some conditions (e.g., 300°C axial creep) may not have been long enough to fully observe the accelerating stage [Zhang 2009, pp. 3-5].
- The hypothesis that hydrostatic stress causes volumetric creep is presented as an assumption for model derivation, based on experimental observation [Zhang 2009, pp. 5-7].

## Assumptions / Conditions
- The fundamental assumption for the 3D visco-elastoplastic problem is that **hydrostatic stress causes volumetric creep** and **differential stress causes axial (shape) creep** [Zhang 2009, pp. 1-3, pp. 5-7].
- The Burgers model is assumed to be a suitable representation for the observed creep behavior of Lu Gray granite under the tested conditions [Zhang 2009, pp. 5-7].

## Key Variables / Parameters
- **Temperature (T):** 300°C, 500°C, 600°C.
- **Stress:** Axial stress (σ1), Confining pressure (σ2=σ3), Hydrostatic stress (σ_h), Differential stress (σ_d = σ1 - σ3).
- **Strain:** Axial strain (ε_a), Volumetric strain (ε_v).
- **Burgers Model Parameters:** Elastic moduli (E_M, E_K) and viscosity coefficients (η_M, η_K) for both Maxwell and Kelvin components, specified separately for axial (superscript *a*) and volumetric (superscript *v*) creep [Zhang 2009, pp. 5-7].

## Reusable Claims
- Under high-temperature 3D stress, Lu Gray granite exhibits clear three-stage (transient, steady-state, accelerating) creep behavior for both axial deformation (at 300-500°C) and volumetric deformation (at 300-600°C) [Zhang 2009, pp. 1-3].
- The volumetric creep of granite under hydrostatic stress is a time-dependent process, challenging the classical assumption that volume deformation is purely elastic [Zhang 2009, pp. 3-5].
- The Burgers viscoelastic model, with parameters derived from creep curve analysis, can adequately simulate the axial and volumetric creep behavior of granite under the tested high-temperature and stress conditions [Zhang 2009, pp. 5-7].
- The quartz α-β phase transition near 573°C is a critical factor that can significantly increase volumetric deformation and promote creep failure in granite at high temperatures [Zhang 2009, pp. 3-5].
