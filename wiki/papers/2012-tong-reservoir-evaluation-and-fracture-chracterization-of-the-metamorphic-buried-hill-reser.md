# Reservoir Evaluation and Fracture Chracterization of the Metamorphic Buried Hill Reservoir in Bohai Bay Basin.

## One-line Summary
This study evaluates the Archean metamorphic buried hill reservoir in the JZ25-1S field by characterizing fracture types, effectiveness, and reservoir zonation to establish classification criteria for development guidance.

## Research Question
How can the fractured reservoirs of the metamorphic buried hill in the JZ25-1S field be accurately evaluated and characterized to guide effective development? [Tong 2012, pp. 1-1]

## Study Area / Data
The study area is the JZ25-1S Oilfield located in the middle part of the Liaoxi low bulge, Liaodong Bay, Bohai Sea. The reservoir is an Archean metamorphic buried hill (migmatization granitic gneiss) in the east high belt, overlain by Paleogene mudstone. [Tong 2012, pp. 1-3]
Data sources include core observation, thin slice identification, imaging logging (FMI), acoustic wavetrain logging, crude oil inclusions testing, and production data from seven wells. [Tong 2012, pp. 1-1, pp. 3-4, pp. 6-7]

## Methods
- **Reservoir Zonation:** Vertical division based on core and logging data into weathered crust, semi-weathered crust, and base rock zones. [Tong 2012, pp. 1-3]
- **Fracture Characterization:** Classification by genesis (diagenetic, weathered dissolved, tectonic) and by angle/filling (early horizontal filled, early vertical filled, late opened inclined, late cataclastic). [Tong 2012, pp. 3-4, pp. 4-6]
- **Fracture Effectiveness Evaluation:**
    1.  Using crude oil inclusions detection technology to assess hydrocarbon presence in different fracture types. [Tong 2012, pp. 4-6]
    2.  Analyzing the relationship between fracture strike and present maximum principal stress direction (NE-SW). [Tong 2012, pp. 4-6]
    3.  Using Stoneley wave reflectivity and energy attenuation from acoustic wavetrain logging to evaluate fracture aperture and permeability. [Tong 2012, pp. 6-7]
- **Reservoir Classification:** Integration of imaging logging interpretation with production data to establish qualitative and semi-quantitative criteria for reservoir levels (I, II, III). [Tong 2012, pp. 6-7, pp. 7-8]

## Key Findings
- The buried hill reservoir is vertically divided into three zones: weathered crust, semi-weathered crust, and base rock. The semi-weathered crust is the most developed and productive zone. [Tong 2012, pp. 1-3, pp. 7-8]
- Reservoir types are categorized as fracture-pore (weathered crust), pore-fracture (semi-weathered crust, main pay zone), and micro fracture-compact (base rock). [Tong 2012, pp. 3-4]
- Low-to-high angle inclined fractures are the dominant fracture type (80%). [Tong 2012, pp. 4-6]
- Late-formed, open inclined fractures parallel to the present maximum geostress (NE-SW) are the most effective for fluid flow. [Tong 2012, pp. 4-6]
- A three-level (I, II, III) reservoir classification system was established based on porosity, fracture development, effectiveness, and production capacity. [Tong 2012, pp. 6-7, pp. 7-8]

## Limitations
- Imaging logging has a limitation in determining the radial extension of fractures. [Tong 2012, pp. 6-7]
- The study relies on data from a limited number of wells (seven) in the JZ25-1S field. [Tong 2012, pp. 1-3]
- The reservoir classification criteria are described as qualitative and semi-quantitative. [Tong 2012, pp. 6-7]

## Assumptions / Conditions
- The effectiveness of natural fractures is assumed to be preserved when their strike is consistent with the present maximum principal stress direction. [Tong 2012, pp. 4-6]
- The granitic gneiss lithology (quartz and plagioclase content 40-70%) is considered conducive to fracture formation under tectonic stress. [Tong 2012, pp. 1-3]
- The buried hill reservoir experienced long-term surface weathering and multi-phase tectonic transformation, leading to secondary reservoir space development. [Tong 2012, pp. 1-1]

## Key Variables / Parameters
- **Reservoir Zones:** Weathered crust, semi-weathered crust, base rock.
- **Reservoir Types:** Fracture-pore, pore-fracture, micro fracture-compact.
- **Fracture Types:** Diagenetic, weathered dissolved, tectonic; classified by angle (horizontal, vertical, inclined).
- **Effectiveness Indicators:** Hydrocarbon inclusion index, angle between fracture strike and present maximum stress, Stoneley wave reflection coefficient and energy attenuation.
- **Classification Parameters:** Porosity (%), fracture development, fracture effectiveness, fracture angle, fracture strike, fracture porosity (%), fracture width (μm), oil production (m³/d).

## Reusable Claims
- In metamorphic buried hill reservoirs, the semi-weathered crust zone typically hosts the most developed and productive reservoirs due to optimal fracture development. [Tong 2012, pp. 1-3]
- Late-formed, open inclined fractures that are parallel to the present-day maximum horizontal stress are the most effective conduits for hydrocarbon flow in fractured basement reservoirs. [Tong 2012, pp. 4-6]
- A combination of crude oil inclusions analysis, geostress orientation mapping, and Stoneley wave logging provides a robust method for qualitatively evaluating fracture effectiveness in crystalline basement rocks. [Tong 2012, pp. 4-6, pp. 6-7]
- Reservoir classification in fractured metamorphic buried hills can be systematically achieved by integrating imaging log-derived fracture parameters with production test data. [Tong 2012, pp. 6-7, pp. 7-8]
