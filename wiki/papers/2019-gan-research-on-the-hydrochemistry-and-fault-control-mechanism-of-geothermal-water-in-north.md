# Research on the Hydrochemistry and Fault Control Mechanism of Geothermal Water in Northwestern Zhangzhou Basin.

## One-line Summary
This study investigates the hydrochemistry of geothermal and cold waters in the Northwestern Zhangzhou Basin, finding that reservoir temperatures (106-148°C) and circulation depths (3.65-5.44 km) are controlled by the Tianbao-Jinshan Fault, with deeper circulation on its northeastern side.

## Research Question
What are the hydrochemical characteristics of geothermal and cold waters in the Northwestern Zhangzhou Basin (NZB), and how do fault structures control the geothermal reservoir temperature and circulation depth? [Gan 2019, pp. 1-2]

## Study Area / Data
The study area is the Northwestern Zhangzhou Basin (NZB) in Fujian Province, China, located at the junction of the Eurasian and Philippine Sea plates. It is part of the southeast coastal volcanic magmatic zone. The main outcrops are Quaternary sediments, late Mesozoic sedimentary rocks, and late Yanshan granites. [Gan 2019, pp. 2-3]
A total of 36 water samples were collected in December 2016, including 7 from geothermal wells, 5 from thermal springs, and 24 cold water samples (wells and springs). [Gan 2019, pp. 3-4]

## Methods
- **Hydrochemical Analysis:** Major cations (K, Na, Ca, Mg) and trace elements were analyzed by ICP-AES and ICP-MS; anions (Cl, SO₄, HCO₃) by Ion Chromatography. [Gan 2019, pp. 3-4]
- **Cluster Analysis (CA):** Hierarchical cluster analysis using the average linkage method on standardized major element data to group water samples. [Gan 2019, pp. 3-4]
- **Geothermometry:** Applied quartz (no steam loss), Na/K, and K/Mg chemical geothermometers to estimate reservoir temperatures. [Gan 2019, pp. 6-7]
- **Silica-Enthalpy Mixing Model:** Used to estimate deep reservoir temperatures and identify mixing processes between geothermal and cold water. [Gan 2019, pp. 9-11]
- **Structural Interpretation:** Remote sensing interpretation to analyze fault control mechanisms. [Gan 2019, pp. 11-12]
- **Circulation Depth Calculation:** Estimated using the formula H = (Tcal - T0)/q + H0, with a geothermal gradient (q) of 23.47 °C/km derived from the HDR-1 well. [Gan 2019, pp. 12-13]

## Key Findings
1.  **Hydrochemical Types:** Geothermal water is of Na-HCO₃·SO₄ type, while cold water is of Ca-Na-HCO₃ type. Low Cl⁻ concentration indicates no seawater mixing in the NZB. [Gan 2019, pp. 4-6, 12-13]
2.  **Reservoir Temperature:** The selected reservoir temperature for the NZB ranges from 106°C to 148°C, determined primarily from the silica-enthalpy mixing model (C2 scenario). Steam loss occurs before mixing with cold water. [Gan 2019, pp. 9-11, 12-13]
3.  **Fault Control:** The Tianbao-Jinshan Fault (TJF) acts as a regional control. Reservoir temperatures differ on its northeastern (NE) and southwestern (SW) sides, attributed to different circulation depths. [Gan 2019, pp. 11-12]
4.  **Circulation Depth:** The circulation depth of geothermal water in the NZB is 3.65–5.44 km, deeper than the central Zhangzhou Basin. The depth on the NE side of the TJF is greater than on the SW side. [Gan 2019, pp. 12-13]
5.  **Mixing:** All geothermal water samples mix with shallow cold water during ascent, as indicated by cluster analysis, Piper diagrams, and the silica-enthalpy model. [Gan 2019, pp. 3-4, 9-11]

## Limitations
- The quartz geothermometer may underestimate temperatures below 150°C due to the presence of other silica minerals or dilution effects. [Gan 2019, pp. 7-9]
- The Na/K geothermometer is less reliable for temperatures below 180°C and can be influenced by cold water dilution and high Ca²⁺ concentrations. [Gan 2019, pp. 7-9]
- The K/Mg geothermometer yields lower temperatures, likely due to mixing with Mg-rich shallow waters. [Gan 2019, pp. 7-9]
- The geothermal gradient (q) used for circulation depth calculation is based on a single deep well (HDR-1) in the central ZB. [Gan 2019, pp. 12-13]
- Cold water samples had charge imbalances >±10% due to low ion concentrations, potentially affecting accuracy. [Gan 2019, pp. 3-4]

## Assumptions / Conditions
- The geothermal water acquires heat solely through deep circulation (geothermal heating), not from recent magmatic activity. [Gan 2019, pp. 11-12]
- The geothermal gradient (23.47 °C/km) from the HDR-1 well is representative of the NZB. [Gan 2019, pp. 12-13]
- The silica-enthalpy mixing model assumes no heat loss after mixing and that samples are not boiling or superheated. [Gan 2019, pp. 9-11]
- The source of geothermal water is meteoric precipitation, as indicated by previous isotope studies. [Gan 2019, pp. 11-12]

## Key Variables / Parameters

- **Circulation Depth (H):** 3.65–5.44 km [Gan 2019, pp. 12-13]
- **Geothermal Gradient (q):** 23.47 °C/km [Gan 2019, pp. 12-13]
- **Mean Annual Air Temperature (T0):** 21°C [Gan 2019, pp. 12-13]
- **Soil Depth of Constant Temperature (H0):** 30 m [Gan 2019, pp. 12-13]
- **Water Temperature:** Geothermal: 42.1–65°C; Cold: 20.8–27.2°C [Gan 2019, pp. 3-4]
- **pH:** Geothermal: 7.7–9.17; Cold: 5.37–7.31 [Gan 2019, pp. 3-4]
- **Major Ions:** Na⁺, HCO₃⁻, SO₄²⁻ dominant in geothermal water; Ca²⁺, HCO₃⁻ dominant in cold water. [Gan 2019, pp. 4-6]

## Reusable Claims
- **Claim:** In the Northwestern Zhangzhou Basin, geothermal reservoir temperatures range from 106°C to 148°C, with steam loss occurring before mixing with shallow cold water.
    - **Condition:** Based on the silica-enthalpy mixing model (C2 scenario) and corroborated by Na-K-Mg geothermometry.
    - **Limitation:** Applicable to the NZB; temperatures may vary in other parts of the basin due to different mixing or fault controls.
- **Claim:** The Tianbao-Jinshan Fault controls geothermal circulation, leading to deeper circulation depths (>4.5 km) and higher reservoir temperatures on its northeastern side compared to its southwestern side (<4.46 km).
    - **Condition:** Inferred from reservoir temperature contour maps and circulation depth calculations.
    - **Limitation:** The fault's exact geometry (dip angle, depth) is not fully constrained.
- **Claim:** The geothermal water in the NZB is of Na-HCO₃·SO₄ type and shows no evidence of seawater mixing, unlike the central Zhangzhou Basin.
    - **Condition:** Based on low Cl⁻ concentrations (<25 mg/L) and hydrochemical analysis.
    - **Limitation:** Does not preclude mixing with other shallow freshwater sources.

## Connections To Other Work
- This study extends previous research focused on the central Zhangzhou Basin to its northwestern periphery. [Gan 2019, pp. 1-2]
- It builds on the established model that the Zhangzhou geothermal system is a fault-controlled convection system heated by deep circulation. [Gan 2019, pp. 1-2]
- The finding of deeper circulation in the NZB (3.65–5.44 km) compared to the central area (3.5–4.0 km) refines earlier estimates. [Gan 2019, pp. 12-13]
- The study uses and references established geothermometer formulations from Fournier, Giggenbach, and Verma & Santoyo. [Gan 2019, pp. 6-7]

## Open Questions
- What is the precise geometry (dip angle, depth extent) of the Tianbao-Jinshan Fault and how does it exactly control fluid flow paths?
- What is the relative contribution of different cold water sources (e.g., local precipitation vs. regional flow) to the mixing observed in geothermal waters?
- How does the geothermal gradient vary spatially across the NZB, and is the value from the central HDR-1 well fully representative?
- What are the long-term sustainable extraction rates for the geothermal resources in the NZB given the identified reservoir temperatures and circulation depths?
