# Formation Mechanism of High-Quality Deep Buried-Hill Reservoir of Archaean Metamorphic Rocks and Its Significance in Petroleum Exploration in Bohai Sea Area.

## One-line Summary
The study identifies lithology, tectonic stress, and weathering as the key controls on the formation of high-quality deep buried-hill reservoirs in Archaean metamorphic rocks of the Bohai Sea, establishing a distribution model for exploration.

## Research Question
What are the formation mechanisms controlling the development of high-quality reservoirs in deep-buried Archaean metamorphic rock buried hills, and what is their significance for petroleum exploration in the Bohai Sea area? [Xu 2020, pp. 1-2]

## Study Area / Data
The study focuses on the BZ 19-6 structure in the southwestern Bozhong Depression, Bohai Sea area. The Archaean metamorphic rock buried hill has a burial depth of 4,000 to 5,500 m. Data include cores, casting thin sections, microscopic fluorescent analysis, scanning electron microscopy (SEM), and conventional physical property measurements from wells such as BZ19-6-1, BZ19-6-2Sa, BZ19-6-3, BZ19-6-4, BZ19-6-7, and BZ19-6-8. [Xu 2020, pp. 1-2, 2-8]

## Methods
The formation mechanism was analyzed using core description, casting thin section observation, microscopic fluorescent analysis of thin sections, scanning electron microscopy (SEM), and conventional physical property measurements (porosity and permeability). A simulation experiment of meteoric freshwater dissolution on granite was conducted at 60°C to study feldspar dissolution. [Xu 2020, pp. 1-2, 8-11]

## Key Findings
1.  The Archaean rocks in the BZ 19-6 structure are dominated by gneiss, metamorphic granite, and migmatite, with high felsic mineral content and strong brittleness, which is prone to fracture development and forms the lithological foundation for reservoirs. [Xu 2020, pp. 1-2, 8-11]
2.  Continuous tectonic fragmentation under stress created large-scale fracture zones and stress fragmenting zones inside the buried hill, which is the key to forming high-quality reservoirs. Three stages of tectonic fractures were identified, with Stage II (Yanshan movement) being the main period for reservoir formation. [Xu 2020, pp. 1-2, 8-11]
3.  Weathering and leaching created secondary dissolution pores and dissolution-enlarged pores along fractures at the top of the buried hill, but deep burial made the top weathering crust relatively dense. The dissolution of feldspar minerals is a key mechanism for secondary pore development. [Xu 2020, pp. 1-2, 8-11]
4.  A reservoir distribution pattern of "vertical connectivity and lateral continuity" was established for large-scale deep metamorphic reservoirs. The reservoir is vertically zoned into a weathered gravel zone, a weathered fracture zone, an inner fracture zone, and a bedrock zone. [Xu 2020, pp. 1-2, 11-13]

## Limitations
The study is focused on a single structure (BZ 19-6) in the Bohai Sea. The complex lithological diversity and multi-stage tectonic history of metamorphic rocks make reservoir evaluation challenging. [Xu 2020, pp. 1-2]

## Assumptions / Conditions
The formation of high-quality reservoirs is primarily controlled by three factors: rock type (lithology), tectonic stress (fracturing), and weathering/leaching (dissolution). The distribution model assumes these controls are consistent across the study area. [Xu 2020, pp. 8-11]

## Key Variables / Parameters
-   **Lithology:** Content of felsic minerals (quartz, feldspar) vs. dark minerals (mica).
-   **Porosity and Permeability:** Measured physical properties of reservoir zones.
-   **Fracture Development:** Stages, density, and fill of tectonic fractures.
-   **Weathering Intensity:** Duration and effect of surface exposure and leaching.
-   **Burial Depth:** 4,000–5,500 m for the studied reservoirs.

## Reusable Claims
-   In deep buried-hill metamorphic reservoirs, the high content of felsic minerals (e.g., in gneiss, metamorphic granite) provides the necessary brittleness for fracture development under tectonic stress, forming the lithological foundation for high-quality reservoirs. [Xu 2020, pp. 1-2, 8-11]
-   Multi-stage tectonic activity, particularly the intense fragmentation during the Yanshan movement, is the key mechanism for creating the extensive fracture networks and cataclastic zones essential for reservoir quality in deep metamorphic rocks. [Xu 2020, pp. 8-11]
-   The dissolution of feldspar minerals along cleavage planes during weathering and leaching is a critical process for generating secondary porosity and enhancing permeability in felsic metamorphic reservoirs. [Xu 2020, pp. 8-11]
-   The vertical zonation of deep metamorphic buried-hill reservoirs (weathered gravel, weathered fracture, inner fracture, bedrock zones) is controlled by the interplay of paleogeomorphology, distance to faults, and the timing of overlying sediment deposition. [Xu 2020, pp. 2-8, 8-11]
