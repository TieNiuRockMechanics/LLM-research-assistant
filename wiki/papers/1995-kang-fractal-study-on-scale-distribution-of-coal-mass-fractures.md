# Fractal Study on Scale Distribution of Coal Mass Fractures.

## One-line Summary
This study proposes and applies two fractal measurement methods to quantify the scale distribution of coal mass fractures, demonstrating their self-similarity and establishing a relationship between fractal dimension and coal metamorphism.

## Research Question
How can the complex, multi-scale distribution of fractures in coal mass be quantitatively described to address the scale effect problem in evaluating coal rock engineering properties? [Kang 1995, pp. 1-3]

## Study Area / Data
Observations and statistics were performed on samples from the main mining coal layers of 11 mines, including Datong, Yangquan, Xishan, Jincheng, and Hebi. The coal layer samples and their characteristics (e.g., vitrinite reflectance, maceral composition) are detailed in a table. [Kang 1995, pp. 3-5]

## Methods
Two fractal measurement methods were proposed and used:
1.  **Fracture Trace Length-Count Fractal Method:** On a polished coal sample plane, the number of fracture traces (N) with length greater than a given scale (r) is counted. If the distribution is fractal, N(r) ∝ r^(-D), where D is the fractal dimension. [Kang 1995, pp. 1-3]
2.  **Grid Scale-Apparent Count Fractal Method:** A square grid of initial scale (r₀) is defined on the sample surface. The number of fractures cutting through the grid is counted. The grid is then subdivided into smaller squares (scale r = r₀/2, r₀/4, etc.), and the cumulative count of fractures cutting through each sub-grid is recorded. The relationship between the grid scale and the apparent count follows a power law. [Kang 1995, pp. 1-3]
A linear correlation and conversion relationship between the fractal dimensions (D₁ and D₂) obtained from these two methods was established. [Kang 1995, pp. 1-3]

## Key Findings
1.  The scale distribution of fractures within coal mass exhibits self-similarity. [Kang 1995, pp. 1-3]
2.  The fractal dimension (D) varies systematically with the degree of coal metamorphism (vitrinite maximum reflectance, R°max). The D value for medium-metamorphic bituminous coal is greater than that for low-metamorphic bituminous coal and high-metamorphic anthracite. [Kang 1995, pp. 3-5]
3.  The fractal dimension of micro-fractures in pure vitrain (D₁) is significantly smaller than that in whole coal (D₂) and macro-fractures (D₃), while D₂ and D₃ are very similar. This indicates that fracture development is influenced by coal lithotype composition. [Kang 1995, pp. 3-5]
4.  The fractal dimension (D) and the number of through-going fractures on a specific surface (N₀) are independent quantitative parameters for characterizing fracture distribution and can be used for engineering classifications, such as predicting top-coal cavability in longwall top-coal caving. [Kang 1995, pp. 5-6]

## Limitations
The study is described as an exploratory research on the variation law of coal mass fracture scale distribution. [Kang 1995, pp. 5-6] The methods require manual counting and statistical analysis on prepared samples.

## Assumptions / Conditions
1.  The fracture distribution is assumed to be fractal (self-similar) for the power-law relationships to hold. [Kang 1995, pp. 1-3]
2.  In the grid scale-apparent count method, the curvature of fracture traces within a grid square is not considered; a fracture cutting the square is counted as one. [Kang 1995, pp. 1-3]
3.  The theoretical derivation of the fracture medium permeability tensor assumes fractures extend infinitely, which is not the case in reality. [Kang 1995, pp. 3-5]

## Key Variables / Parameters
*   **Fractal Dimension (D, D₁, D₂, D₃):** The quantitative measure of the fracture scale distribution complexity. D₁ is for vitrain micro-fractures, D₂ for whole coal micro-fractures, D₃ for macro-fractures. [Kang 1995, pp. 3-5]
*   **Scale (r, r₀):** The measurement length or grid size used in the fractal analysis. [Kang 1995, pp. 1-3]
*   **Count (N, N(r), N₀):** The number of fractures at or above a given scale. N₀ is the number of through-going fractures on a specific surface. [Kang 1995, pp. 1-3, 5-6]
*   **Vitrinite Maximum Reflectance (R°max):** An indicator of coal metamorphism degree. [Kang 1995, pp. 3-5]
*   **Fractal Ratio:** The ratio of a smaller grid scale to the initial scale (r/r₀). [Kang 1995, pp. 1-3]

## Reusable Claims
