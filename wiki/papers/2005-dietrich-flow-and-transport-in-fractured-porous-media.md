# Flow and Transport in Fractured Porous Media

## One-line Summary

This edited volume presents research from the German DFG Collaborative Research Center (SFB 405) on characterizing, modeling, and understanding flow and transport processes in fractured porous media, integrating field investigations, laboratory experiments, and numerical modeling approaches.

## Research Question

The book addresses fundamental questions about how fluids and solutes move through fractured rock systems, including: How can hydraulic properties of fractured rocks be characterized? How do flow and transport processes operate in fractured media at various scales? How can numerical models be developed and validated to simulate these complex systems? [Dietrich 2005, pp. 1-5]

## Study Area / Data

The research initiative was launched in 1999 and funded by the German Research Foundation (DFG) as Collaborative Research Center SFB 405. The work involves multiple German universities and research institutions: University of Tübingen, University of Stuttgart, University of Göttingen, University of Karlsruhe, RWTH Aachen University, and the Environmental Research Center (UFZ) Leipzig. [Dietrich 2005, pp. 5-6]

The book contains 306 figures and 65 tables. [Dietrich 2005, pp. 1-5]

## Key Findings

The book presents findings organized into several major parts:

1. **Characterization of hydraulic properties** of fractured rocks, including methods for determining fracture geometry and hydraulic parameters [Dietrich 2005, pp. 8-9]

2. **Flow processes in fractured media**, covering single-phase and multiphase flow in fracture networks [Dietrich 2005, pp. 9-11]

3. **Transport processes** including solute transport, reactive transport, and density-dependent flow in fractured systems [Dietrich 2005, pp. 11-14]

4. **Numerical modeling** of flow and transport, including discrete fracture network models and continuum approaches [Dietrich 2005, pp. 14-17]

5. **Scale effects** and the challenge of representing fractured media at different spatial scales [Dietrich 2005, pp. 6-8]
