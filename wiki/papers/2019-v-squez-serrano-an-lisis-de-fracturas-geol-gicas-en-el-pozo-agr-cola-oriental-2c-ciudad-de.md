# Análisis de fracturas geológicas en el pozo Agrícola Oriental 2C, Ciudad de México y su relación con fallas mayores.

## One-line Summary
This study analyzes fractures in a volcanic rock core from the Agrícola Oriental 2C well in Mexico City, identifying two fracture families and relating them to a major fault, with fractal analysis revealing patterns altered by pre-existing fractures.

## Research Question
How are geological fractures in the volcanic rocks of the Agrícola Oriental 2C well characterized, and what is their relationship to major faults in the Mexico Basin? [Serrano 2019, pp. 1-1]

## Study Area / Data
The study focuses on the Agrícola Oriental 2C well (19°23'43.25"N, 99° 5'11.01"W) in the Iztacalco municipality of Mexico City. A continuous 9-meter core (N2AO2C) was recovered from 1550 to 1559 m depth, near the contact between Cretaceous limestone and Oligocene volcanic rocks at ~1560 m depth. The core consists of altered andesitic volcanic rock. [Serrano 2019, pp. 1-1, 2-3, 6-6]

## Methods
Fracture analysis was performed in one dimension (1D) along the core using a scan line method. Data on fracture spacing and aperture were collected without differentiating between fracture families to observe the effect of superposition. The core was divided into nine 1-meter segments for parameter variation analysis. Parameters calculated include:
- Fracture intensity (number of fractures per unit length)
- Box fractal dimension (Dc)
- Correlation dimension (υ)
- Lyapunov exponent (Lv)
- Coefficient of variation (Cv)
- Cumulative frequency exponent (Efa) for spacing and aperture
Calculations were performed using spreadsheets and the Frac1Dv1.1 program written in Matlab. [Serrano 2019, pp. 6-6, 7-8, 8-8]

## Key Findings
1. Two fracture families were identified: F1 (older, filled with calcite) and F2 (younger, open or filled with clays/oxides, related to the main fault). [Serrano 2019, pp. 1-1, 6-6]
2. Fracture spacing and aperture follow a power law in log-log cumulative frequency plots, indicating fractal behavior. [Serrano 2019, pp. 1-1, 8-9]
3. Parameters like fracture intensity, box fractal dimension, Lyapunov exponent, and aperture Efa generally decrease with increasing distance from the fault (1556-1559 m depth). In contrast, the coefficient of variation and spacing Efa increase with distance. [Serrano 2019, pp. 1-1, 9-10, 10-11]
4. The expected patterns are altered between 1550-1556 m depth due to the superposition of the pre-existing F1 fracture family, which increases fracture quantity and redistributes their arrangement. [Serrano 2019, pp. 1-1, 10-11, 12-14]
5. The fault encountered is informally named the Agrícola Oriental fault. Based on fracture characteristics and regional tectonics, it is interpreted to belong to the northern sector of the Santa Catarina graben with an ENE-WSW orientation. [Serrano 2019, pp. 1-1, 12-14, 14-14]

## Limitations
- The recovered core was not oriented, preventing direct determination of absolute fracture orientations. [Serrano 2019, pp. 12-14]
- The study is based on a single 9-meter core from one well, limiting spatial representation. [Serrano 2019, pp. 6-6]
- No cores were recovered from the limestone near the contact, so its fracture state is unknown. [Serrano 2019, pp. 2-3]
- The age of the volcanic rocks in the AO2C well is inferred from the nearby Mixhuca well, not directly dated. [Serrano 2019, pp. 4-6]

## Assumptions / Conditions
- The stratigraphic column from the nearby Mixhuca well is representative for the AO2C well. [Serrano 2019, pp. 4-6]
- The volcanic rocks in the core are of probable Oligocene age, belonging to the Lower Volcanic Unit (UVI). [Serrano 2019, pp. 4-6, 14-14]
- The two fracture families (F1 and F2) represent two distinct deformation events related to different regional fault systems. [Serrano 2019, pp. 12-14, 14-14]

## Reusable Claims
- In a fault damage zone, parameters such as fracture intensity, box fractal dimension, Lyapunov exponent, and aperture cumulative frequency exponent typically decrease with increasing distance from the fault, while the coefficient of variation and spacing cumulative frequency exponent increase. [Serrano 2019, pp. 1-1, 10-11]
- The superposition of a pre-existing fracture system (F1) on a newer system (F2) within a shear zone can increase the total fracture intensity and cause anomalous, non-systematic variations in geometric and distributional parameters with distance from the fault. [Serrano 2019, pp. 1-1, 10-11, 12-14]
- Fracture spacing and aperture distributions in the studied volcanic rocks follow a power law, indicating fractal behavior. [Serrano 2019, pp. 1-1, 8-9]
- The contact between Cretaceous limestone and Oligocene volcanic rocks in the central Mexico Basin can be tectonic (a normal fault) rather than a simple unconformity. [Serrano 2019, pp. 12-14, 14-14]

## Connections To Other Work
- The study builds on previous stratigraphic and structural work in the Mexico Basin by Pérez-Cruz (1988) and Arce et al. (2013, 2015). [Serrano 2019, pp. 2-3, 4-6]
- It relates the findings to the major fault systems (NNW-SSE and ENE-WSW) documented in the Trans-Mexican Volcanic Belt by Alaniz-Álvarez et al. (1998) and García-Palomo et al. (2008). [Serrano 2019, pp. 6-6, 12-14]
