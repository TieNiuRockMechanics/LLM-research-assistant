# Real-Time Permeability Evolution of Thermally Cracked Granite at Triaxial Stresses.

## One-line Summary
This study identifies 300°C as the threshold temperature for a sharp, exponential increase in the permeability of Luhui granite under triaxial stresses, linking this change directly to the interconnection of thermally induced micro-cracks.

## Research Question
What is the threshold temperature for permeability change in thermally cracked granite under triaxial stress conditions, and how does the evolution of micro-cracks relate to this permeability change?

## Study Area / Data
The study used Luhui granite samples mined from a strip mine in Shandong province, China [Feng 2018, pp. 3-6]. The granite is composed of 28% quartz, 43% feldspar, 25% illite, and 4% others, with an initial porosity of 0.3%-0.55% [Feng 2018, pp. 3-6]. Samples were cylindrical, 200mm in diameter and 400mm long [Feng 2018, pp. 3-6].

## Methods
Permeability was measured in real-time at elevated temperatures (up to 600°C) and triaxial stresses (25MPa and 75MPa) using a 600℃20MN servo-controlled triaxial rock mechanics testing machine [Feng 2018, pp. 1-3, 3-6]. The steady-state flow method was employed with nitrogen as the pore fluid to minimize chemical effects, and permeability was calculated using Darcy's Law [Feng 2018, pp. 6-8]. The evolution of meso-cracks at different temperatures was observed using an MPV micro-photometer [Feng 2018, pp. 6-8].

## Key Findings
- 300°C is the threshold temperature (Tc) for permeability change in thermally cracked granite under triaxial stresses [Feng 2018, pp. 1-3, 13-16].
- Below 300°C, permeability magnitude is 10⁻⁷ Darcy (D) with a low increase [Feng 2018, pp. 1-3].
- Between 300°C and 400°C, permeability magnitude increases drastically to 10⁻⁶ D [Feng 2018, pp. 1-3].
- At 400°C, permeability magnitude reaches 10⁻⁵ D [Feng 2018, pp. 1-3].
- Two peaks in micro-crack quantity (for lengths >5μm and >10μm) were observed up to 400°C, with the peak for larger cracks occurring at higher temperatures [Feng 2018, pp. 8-11].
- The sharp increase in permeability above 300°C is attributed to the drastic increase and interconnection of micro-cracks, particularly those longer than 10μm [Feng 2018, pp. 1-3, 11-13].
- Thermal and mechanical coupled effect affects granite permeability more heavily than only thermal effect [Feng 2018, pp. 1-3, 13-16].

## Limitations
- The large temperature increment (50°C) between measurement points did not allow for a detailed determination of the critical temperature scope, though it is inferred to be narrow around 300°C [Feng 2018, pp. 11-13].
- The debate on the existence of a theoretical threshold temperature for thermal cracking itself is acknowledged, as it may be limited by the sensitivity of detection equipment like acoustic emission [Feng 2018, pp. 8-11, 11-13].

## Assumptions / Conditions
- The flow of nitrogen through the granite samples was considered laminar, as the Reynolds number was maintained below 5 [Feng 2018, pp. 6-8].
- The use of nitrogen as pore fluid minimized chemical reactions with the granite [Feng 2018, pp. 3-6].
- The bonding force in rock is complex and ranges from very weak to very strong, allowing for crack initiation at the beginning of heating [Feng 2018, pp. 8-11].

## Key Variables / Parameters
- **Threshold Temperature (Tc):** 300°C for permeability change [Feng 2018, pp. 1-3].
- **Permeability (k):** Measured in Darcy (D), ranging from 10⁻⁷ D to 10⁻⁵ D [Feng 2018, pp. 1-3].
- **Confining Pressure:** 25 MPa and 75 MPa, simulating different burial depths [Feng 2018, pp. 6-8].
- **Micro-crack Length:** Categories of >5μm and >10μm were analyzed [Feng 2018, pp. 8-11].

## Reusable Claims
- Under triaxial stress conditions, the permeability of thermally cracked Luhui granite exhibits a sharp, exponential increase when temperature exceeds 300°C, transitioning from a magnitude of 10⁻⁷ D to 10⁻⁵ D [Feng 2018, pp. 1-3, 11-13].
- The interconnection of micro-cracks, particularly those longer than 10μm, is the primary mechanism for the drastic permeability increase above the 300°C threshold in granite [Feng 2018, pp. 1-3, 11-13].
- Real-time permeability measurement under coupled thermal and mechanical loading reveals a lower threshold temperature (300°C) compared to measurements taken after preheating and cooling (~500°C), indicating that coupled effects accelerate damage and permeability evolution [Feng 2018, pp. 13-16].

## Connections To Other Work
- The study builds on prior work that identified a thermal cracking threshold temperature of 60-70°C in Westerly granite using acoustic emission [Feng 2018, pp. 3-6, 8-11].
- It addresses a prediction by Chen et al. that the permeability threshold temperature for granite would be higher than 200°C [Feng 2018, pp. 3-6, 11-13].
- The findings contrast with studies that measured permeability after preheating and cooling, which found abrupt changes at higher temperatures (~500°C) [Feng 2018, pp. 13-16].

## Open Questions
- Does the threshold temperature for permeability change vary significantly with different granite types, mineral compositions, or initial crack structures?
- What is the precise, narrow temperature scope around 300°C where the permeability saltation occurs?
- How do the findings translate to other crystalline rocks or under different stress paths?
