# Mechanical Behavior and Acoustic Emission Characteristics of Intact Granite Undergoing Direct Shear.

## One-line Summary
This study investigates the shear behavior and acoustic emission (AE) characteristics of intact granite samples from a deep geothermal well, identifying three shear stages and characteristic stresses, and comparing the AE evolution with that of rock fractures.

## Research Question
The main aim is to reveal the acoustic emission (AE) characteristics of intact granite samples under direct shear with constant normal stress, and to examine the correlation between shear-induced fracturing and AE parameters [Shang 2021, pp. 1-2].

## Study Area / Data
The tested granite samples were produced from cores recovered from a deep geothermal exploration well in Laoting, Hebei, China, at depths of about 3200–3600 m. Two cubic samples with a side length of 50 mm were prepared from rock cores at depths of 3298 m (sample G32) and 3620 m (sample G36) [Shang 2021, pp. 2-3].

## Methods
- **Sample Preparation:** Cubic granite samples were polished to a surface roughness of ±3 μm [Shang 2021, pp. 2-3].
- **Direct Shear Test:** Used the YZW-50 apparatus. A constant normal stress of 5 MPa was applied first, followed by a constant shear rate of 0.2 mm/min [Shang 2021, pp. 2-3].
- **Acoustic Emission (AE) Monitoring:** An AE signal detecting unit (SIEMENS) with six PAC Micro-30 sensors (resonant frequency 125 kHz, operating range 150–400 kHz) and a six-channel PCI-2 system was used. The trigger threshold was set to 40 dB, and full-waveform data were recorded at 0.5 MHz. AE parameters (event, ring down count, energy) were monitored following the ISRM suggested method [Shang 2021, pp. 2-3].
- **Surface Scanning:** A 3D blue light scanner (OKIO-5M, TENYOUN) was used to measure the fracture surfaces after shear [Shang 2021, pp. 2-3].

## Key Findings
1.  Three shear stages were identified from shear stress-displacement and cumulative AE parameter-displacement curves: compaction, quasi-elastic, and failure phases. The transition from quasi-elastic to failure is marked by a slight drop in shear stress [Shang 2021, pp. 3-6, 8-9].
2.  Three characteristic stresses were defined for intact rock under shear: crack initiation stress (τ_ci), crack damage stress (τ_cd), and peak strength (τ_p). τ_ci is about 0.80 times the peak strength, and τ_cd is about 0.88 times the peak strength [Shang 2021, pp. 8-9].
3.  AE parameters showed three evolution stages: a "quiet" stage, a stable growth stage, and a rapid increase stage, corresponding to the three shear stages [Shang 2021, pp. 8-9].
4.  AE events were scattered in the pre-peak stage but localized around the shear band in the post-peak stage. High-energy AE events appeared concentrated near the shear band before the peak stress [Shang 2021, pp. 3-6].
5.  The AE b-value exhibited three stages: initial high values, continuous decrease until the drop in shear stress, and a sharp decrease to low values after failure [Shang 2021, pp. 6-8].
6.  Compared to rock fractures, where AE activity peaks at the peak shear stress due to asperity damage, intact rocks show a first rapid increase in cumulative AE parameters before the peak stress, indicating crack coalescence and shear band formation [Shang 2021, pp. 8-9].

## Limitations
- The study tested only two intact granite samples [Shang 2021, pp. 2-3].
- The research focuses on intact granite under a specific constant normal stress (5 MPa) and shear rate (0.2 mm/min) [Shang 2021, pp. 2-3].
- The comparison with rock fractures is based on referenced studies, not direct parallel experiments in this work [Shang 2021, pp. 8-9].

## Assumptions / Conditions
- Tests were conducted under a constant normal stress of 5 MPa [Shang 2021, pp. 2-3].
- The shear rate was maintained at a constant 0.2 mm/min [Shang 2021, pp. 2-3].
- Sample surfaces were polished to ±3 μm to avoid end effects [Shang 2021, pp. 2-3].
- AE source location accuracy was enhanced using six sensors, with a minimum location error below 2 mm [Shang 2021, pp. 2-3].

## Reusable Claims
- For intact granite under direct shear with a constant normal stress of 5 MPa, the crack initiation stress is approximately 0.80 times the peak shear strength, and the crack damage stress is approximately 0.88 times the peak shear strength [Shang 2021, pp. 8-9].
- The evolution of cumulative AE parameters (events, ring down counts, energy) with shear displacement follows an "S" shape, with distinct quiet, stable growth, and rapid increase phases that correlate with the compaction, quasi-elastic, and failure shear stages [Shang 2021, pp. 3-6, 8-9].
- In intact rock samples undergoing shear, AE events are scattered in the pre-peak stage but become localized along the developing shear band in the post-peak stage, contrasting with rock fractures where AE events localize around the fracture surface post-peak [Shang 2021, pp. 3-6, 8-9].
- The first rapid increase point in cumulative AE parameters before the peak shear stress in intact rock is a useful indicator for predicting the formation of shear bands and potential instability in deep reservoir rocks [Shang 2021, pp. 8-9].
