# Scaling of Fracture Patterns in Three Deep Boreholes and Implications for Constraining Fractal Discrete Fracture Network Models.

## One-line Summary
This study develops a methodology for generating fractal fracture networks and demonstrates that the two-point correlation function provides the most reliable estimate of the fractal dimension of fracture spacing from 1D borehole data, revealing consistent fractal scaling in deep crystalline rock reservoirs.

## Research Question
How can the scaling properties of fracture networks be characterized from 1D borehole data, and what are the limitations of using such data to constrain 2D and 3D fractal discrete fracture network (DFN) models? [Afshari 2019, pp. 2-3]

## Study Area / Data
The study analyzes fracture data from three deep geothermal boreholes: the Basel-1 well in Basel, Switzerland, and the GPK3 and GPK4 wells at the Soultz-sous-Forêts site in France. All wells penetrate crystalline basement rock to depths of approximately 5 km. [Afshari 2019, pp. 14-16] Fracture orientations were derived from acoustic televiewer logs, with fractures assigned to sets based on orientation. [Afshari 2019, pp. 14-16]

## Methods
1.  **Synthetic DFN Generation:** A step-by-step methodology is presented for generating 1D, 2D, and 3D fractal fracture networks that respect the dual power-law model (Eq. 1). This model is defined by two independent parameters: the correlation dimension (D) governing the spatial distribution of fracture centers and the length exponent (a) governing the fracture length distribution. [Afshari 2019, pp. 2-3, 3-4]
2.  **Multiplicative Cascade Process:** Fractal density distributions of fracture centers are generated using a multiplicative cascade process. Fracture centers are then assigned using a discrete inverse transform method, and lengths are assigned according to the power-law distribution. [Afshari 2019, pp. 4-5, 5-6]
3.  **Stereological Analysis:** Synthetic 2D and 3D DFNs were generated to evaluate the stereological relationships between scaling parameters in different dimensions (e.g., relating 1D scanline data to 2D/3D network parameters). [Afshari 2019, pp. 9-10]
4.  **Fractal Dimension Estimation Techniques:** Three techniques were applied to synthetic 1D data of known fractal dimension and to natural borehole data: the two-point correlation function, the box-counting technique, and the power-law distribution of spacing. [Afshari 2019, pp. 12-13, 13-14]
5.  **Application to Borehole Data:** The two-point correlation function was applied to fracture intersection depths from the three deep boreholes to estimate the 1D correlation dimension (D1D) for all fractures and for individual fracture sets. [Afshari 2019, pp. 16-18]

## Key Findings
1.  The two-point correlation function provides the most stable and reliable estimate of the fractal dimension for 1D fracture spacing data, outperforming box-counting and power-law spacing distribution methods. [Afshari 2019, pp. 13-14, 16-18]
2.  Fracture spacing distributions along all three deep boreholes are fractal over more than two orders of magnitude in scale (typically 2–1000 m). [Afshari 2019, pp. 16-18]
3.  For all fractures combined in each well, the estimated 1D correlation dimension (D1D) is very consistent, ranging from 0.86 to 0.88. [Afshari 2019, pp. 16-18]
4.  For individual fracture sets of common orientation, the fractal scaling is also observed over more than two orders of magnitude, with D1D values ranging between 0.65 and 0.75. [Afshari 2019, pp. 16-18]
5.  Stereological analysis of synthetic networks shows that it is **not possible** to estimate the 2D or 3D fractal scaling parameters (D2D, D3D, a) from the 1D correlation dimension (D1D) of fracture spacing from boreholes, even if the length exponent (a) is known a priori. The problem is underdetermined. [Afshari 2019, pp. 9-10, 19-20]

## Limitations
1.  The stereological problem of constraining 2D and 3D fracture network scaling parameters from 1D borehole data is strongly underdetermined and leads to non-unique solutions. [Afshari 2019, pp. 19-20]
2.  The acoustic televiewer logs used may not identify very thin discontinuities, potentially affecting the completeness of the fracture dataset. [Afshari 2019, pp. 14-16]
3.  The analysis assumes fracture networks can be considered as fractal objects. [Afshari 2019, pp. 16-18]
4.  The Levy-Lee flight DFN generation method was found to produce networks where the output fractal dimension did not match the input, likely due to finite domain effects. [Afshari 2019, pp. 3-4, 16-18]

## Assumptions / Conditions
1.  The fracture networks in the studied rock masses follow a fractal organization. [Afshari 2019, pp. 16-18]
2.  The dual power-law model (Eq. 1) is a valid representation for generating synthetic fractal DFNs. [Afshari 2019, pp. 2-3]
3.  The two-point correlation function is the most reliable method for estimating the fractal dimension of 1D fracture spacing data. [Afshari 2019, pp. 13-14]
4.  The fracture datasets from the boreholes provide a reasonably complete sampling of the fracture families present. [Afshari 2019, pp. 14-16]

## Key Variables / Parameters
*   **D (Correlation Dimension):** Parameter governing the spatial distribution (clustering) of fracture centers. [Afshari 2019, pp. 1-2]
*   **a (Length Exponent):** Parameter governing the power-law distribution of fracture lengths. [Afshari 2019, pp. 1-2]
*   **D1D:** The 1D correlation dimension estimated from fracture spacing along a scanline or borehole. [Afshari 2019, pp. 9-10]
*   **D2D, D3D:** The correlation dimension of fracture centers in 2D and 3D, respectively. [Afshari 2019, pp. 9-10]
*   **a2D, a3D:** The length exponent in 2D and 3D, respectively. [Afshari 2019, pp. 9-10]
*   **C(r):** The two-point correlation function. [Afshari 2019, pp. 3-4]

## Reusable Claims
1.  **Claim:** The two-point correlation function provides a more stable and reliable estimate of the fractal dimension for 1D fracture spacing data than box-counting or power-law spacing distribution methods.
    *   **Condition:** Based on tests with synthetic 1D data of known fractal dimension.
    *   **Limitation:** Valid for characterizing the spatial clustering of fracture intersection points along a line.
2.  **Claim:** Fracture spacing distributions in deep crystalline rock reservoirs (Basel, Soultz-sous-Forêts) exhibit fractal scaling over more than two orders of magnitude, with a correlation dimension (D1D) of approximately 0.86-0.88 for all fractures.
    *   **Condition:** Derived from borehole image log data in three ~5 km deep wells.
    *   **Limitation:** Represents the 1D sampling of the 3D network; direct extrapolation to 3D parameters is not possible.
3.  **Claim:** It is not possible to uniquely determine the 2D or 3D fractal scaling parameters (D and a) of a fracture network solely from the 1D correlation dimension (D1D) measured along a borehole, even if the length exponent (a) is known.
    *   **Condition:** Demonstrated through stereological analysis of synthetic fractal DFNs.
    *   **Limitation:** The inverse problem is underdetermined; additional information (e.g., from induced seismicity or stress tomography) is required.
