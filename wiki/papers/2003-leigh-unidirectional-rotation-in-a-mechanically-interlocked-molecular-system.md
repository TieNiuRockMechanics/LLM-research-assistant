# Unidirectional Rotation in a Mechanically Interlocked Molecular System.

## One-line Summary
The paper demonstrates and analyzes unidirectional rotation of a macrocycle within a [3]catenane system driven by light-induced isomerization, with kinetic studies on model compounds supporting the directionality of motion.

## Research Question
The study aims to show that the components of a [3]catenane undergo unidirectional rotation over a range of conditions and to estimate the contributions of competing processes using kinetics of model compounds [Leigh 2003, pp. 1-1].

## Study Area / Data
The research focuses on a synthetic [3]catenane system (compound 3) and model symmetrical two-station [2]rotaxanes. Data were obtained from variable-temperature 1H NMR spectroscopy in CDCl3 and molecular modelling [Leigh 2003, pp. 1-1].

## Methods
Kinetic studies were performed on model [2]rotaxanes using variable-temperature 1H NMR spectroscopy to experimentally determine energy barriers for macrocycle movement between different stations. Molecular modelling was used to attribute changes in dynamics to hydrogen-bonding patterns [Leigh 2003, pp. 1-1].

## Key Findings
The benzylic amide macrocycle in the [3]catenane moves unidirectionally due to large differences in energy barriers for decomplexation from various stations (e.g., fumaramide: 16.2 kcal mol⁻¹, succinic amide ester: 11.3 kcal mol⁻¹, amide: 8 kcal mol⁻¹). The kinetics of model compounds suggest overwhelming directionality for stimuli-induced motion in the [3]catenane over a wide range of conditions [Leigh 2003, pp. 1-1].

## Limitations
It is acknowledged that inter-station dynamics in the model compounds may differ from those in the [3]catenane [Leigh 2003, pp. 1-1].

## Assumptions / Conditions
The analysis assumes that the 'blocking' ring in the [3]catenane is effectively translationally immobile over the time period that the other ring moves. The kinetics of model [2]rotaxanes are assumed to be representative of the dynamics within the [3]catenane [Leigh 2003, pp. 1-1].

## Reusable Claims
- In a [3]catenane system, unidirectional rotation of a macrocycle can be achieved by creating large differences in the energy barriers for decomplexation from different stations, which can be tuned via photoisomerization [Leigh 2003, pp. 1-1].
- Kinetic studies on model [2]rotaxanes can be used to estimate the directionality of stimuli-induced motion in more complex mechanically interlocked molecules like [3]catenanes [Leigh 2003, pp. 1-1].
- The use of stimuli-induced mechanical movement in one component to influence dynamics in another is a principle underpinning macroscopic machines, though it remains rare in synthetic molecular systems [Leigh 2003, pp. 1-1].
